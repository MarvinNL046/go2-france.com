Napoleon Bonaparte, the 19th-century French emperor, commissioned the original Arc de Triomphe in 1806 to honor his military victories, which stands as a enduring symbol of French nationalism on the Champs-Élysées. Widely known public history notes that such arches were common in ancient Rome and adopted by European leaders to project dominance.

Trump's version, as reported, would be a modern iteration, potentially featuring inscriptions of his administration's milestones.
