## Frequently Asked Questions

### Do I need an eSIM for France if I'm from the EU?
If you're from the EU, your home SIM works under roaming rules with no extra fees for up to 30 days. Non-EU travelers benefit most from eSIMs to avoid high charges—plans start at €5 for 1GB.

### How do I install an eSIM on my iPhone for France travel?
Go to Settings > Cellular > Add Cellular Plan, scan the QR code from your provider. It takes under 5 minutes and activates upon landing; ensure your iPhone is model 12 or later for compatibility. For Android, use similar steps in Connections > SIM Card Manager.

### What's the best eSIM data amount for a 10-day France trip?
For moderate use like maps and social media, 10-20GB covers most needs across cities like Paris and rural spots. Heavy streamers may need unlimited; track usage via your provider's app to avoid surprises.

### Can I use my eSIM in rural France areas like Provence?
Yes, eSIMs connect to local networks with 95% 4G coverage nationwide, though speeds may slow in remote Provence hills. Download offline resources for hikes; signal improves near main roads.

### Is there free internet at French airports and train stations?
Major airports like CDG offer free WiFi for 2 hours, and SNCF stations provide unlimited access with registration. However, for security, use eSIM data instead—link to our [Transport](https://go2-france.com/transport/) guide for more.

### What if my eSIM doesn't work in France—how to fix it?
Restart your phone, check APN settings per provider instructions, or contact support via email. If all fails, buy a local prepaid at a tabac shop for €10-€20; prevention via pre-testing is key.

### Are eSIMs safe for banking and sensitive apps in France?
Absolutely, as they use encrypted local networks—safer than public WiFi. Always enable a VPN for extra protection, especially in crowded tourist areas like the [Pont Du Gard](https://go2-france.com/destinations/pont-du-gard/).
